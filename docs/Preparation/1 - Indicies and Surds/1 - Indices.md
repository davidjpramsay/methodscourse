## Notes

### Introduction

Indices, or powers, represent repeated multiplication in the same way multiplication represents repeated addition.

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
a \times a \times a \times a = a^4
$$
    
</div>
</details>

### Indices Laws

#### Multiplication of the same base.

$$
a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}
$$

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
\begin{align*}
x^5 \times x^3 &= x^{5+3}\\
&=x^8
\end{align*}
$$
    
</div>
</details>

#### Division of the same base

$$
\displaystyle{\frac{a^m}{a^n}} = a^{m-n}
$$

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{y^7}{y^3}&=y^{7-3}\\
&=y^{4}
\end{align*}
$$
    
</div>
</details>

#### Power to a power.

$$
\left( \frac{a^m}{b^n} \right)^p = \frac{a^{mp}}{b^{np}}
$$

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
\begin{align*}
\left(\frac{2p^2}{q^3}\right)^3 &= \left(\frac{2^3p^{2 \times 3}}{q^{3 \times 3}}\right)\\
&=\frac{8p^6}{q^9}
\end{align*}
$$
    
</div>
</details>

#### Power of zero identity.

$$
a^0 = 1
$$

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
\begin{align*}
\left(3q^2-4\right)^0 + 3 &= 1 + 3\\
&=4
\end{align*}
$$
    
</div>
</details>

#### Negative powers.

$$
a^{-1} = \frac{1}{a}
$$

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
\begin{align*}
\left( \frac{a}{b^2} \right)^{-1} &= \frac{a^{-1}}{b^{-4}} \\
&= \frac{b^{4}}{a}\\
\end{align*}
$$

</div>
</details>

#### Fractional powers.

$$
a^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[m]{a^n}
$$

<details>
<summary>Example</summary>
<div>

$$
\begin{align*}
4^{\frac{3}{2}} &= \left( \sqrt{4} \right)^3\\
&= 2^3\\
&= 8
\end{align*}
$$

In this example you could have cubed $4$ before square rooting, however; as a general rule it is better to **make numbers smaller before you make them larger**.

</div>
</details>
